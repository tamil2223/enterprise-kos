RBAC + tenant isolation (golden corpus)

Enterprise correctness requires that the LLM never sees data the user is not permitted to access.

In KOS, RBAC should be enforced at the MCP context gateway boundary: context items are filtered by (user, role, tenant) before they are returned to agents or injected into prompts.

Typical controls:
- Role-based access checks (who can read what)
- Tenant scoping (cross-tenant leakage must be impossible)
- Auditing (who requested which context and why)
